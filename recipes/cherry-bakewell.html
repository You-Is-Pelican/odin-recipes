Cherry Bakewell

A Recipe for a Plant-Based Cherry Bakewell!

This is my favouite cake. There's little more to say.
The pairing of cherry and almond is nothing new,
but the way this recipe balances these flavours,
how they are suspended in the richest and fluffiest of sponges is.
Or at least it was to me. Give it a go.

Ingredients

  • 230 g (1 + ¾ cups + 2 Tbsp) plain (all-purpose) flour
  • 175 g (6 oz) ground almonds
  • 230 g (1 cup + 2 Tbsp) caster (superfine) sugar
  • 3 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 140 ml (½ cup + 4 tsp) neutral oil (I like sunflower, olive or avocado)
  • 350 ml (1 + ⅓ cups + 2 Tbsp) unsweetened non-dairy milk (soy is best)
  • 1 tsp almond extract (or vanilla)
  • 1 Tbsp cider or white wine vinegar (or lemon juice)
  • 250 g (9 oz) pitted cherries (frozen or fresh)
  • a couple of handfuls of flaked almonds

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C/160°C fan/350°F/gas mark 4. Grease a 23 x 33 cm / 9 x 13 inch rectangular cake tin and line it with baking parchment
  2. Place the flour, ground almonds, sugar, baking powder and salt in a large bowl and stir with a balloon whisk until well combined
  3. Add the oil, milk, almond extract and vinegar and whisk until no dry lumps remain. Take care not to over-beat the batter
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared tin and spread it level. Scatter over the cherries and flaked almonds
  5. Bake the cake for about 35 minutes until pale golden and a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean
  6. Leave the cake to cool completely in the tin before slicing it
  7. Tell your friends!
